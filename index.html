The early weeks are harder than anyone prepared you for.

You're recovering from birth. You're learning to feed a baby who didn't read the manual. You haven't slept properly in days. And the person you most want to call lives hundreds of kilometres away.

You know help exists somewhere — but finding it, vetting it, and coordinating it while you're running on empty? That's not something you should have to do alone.

We do what your mum would do if she lived around the corner.

Your coordinator learns your situation, works out what you need, and puts the right people around you. They introduce you to providers they personally trust, handle the scheduling, and check in every week to make sure things are working. If they're not, they adjust.

You never have to explain your situation twice. You never have to chase a booking. You never have to wonder if you've chosen the right person.

We handle the coordination. You focus on your baby and yourself.

Three steps. No overwhelm.

Step one

Tell us where you're at

Answer a few short questions about your situation — when your baby arrived or is due, where you live, and what feels hardest right now. It takes about three minutes. No account to create. Nothing to commit to.

Step two

We build your plan

A member of our team gets in touch within a few hours. We listen, ask the right questions, and put together a support plan that fits your family and your budget — with providers we've personally chosen for your situation.

Step three

Your village begins

We introduce you to your providers, coordinate the scheduling, and stay in touch to make sure everything is working. As your needs change, your plan changes with you. We're in your corner for as long as you need us.

How we earn your trust.

Inviting someone into your home during the most vulnerable weeks of your life is not a small thing. We take that seriously.

Every provider is personally vetted.

Qualifications checked. References contacted. A conversation with our team to assess not just competence but warmth, sensitivity, and how they treat families under pressure. We don't accept everyone who applies. Our vetting process was developed in consultation with practising perinatal professionals.

Your coordinator knows your story.

You're not a number. Your coordinator reads what you've told us, understands your context, and remembers it between conversations. If something isn't right, they fix it before you have to ask.

We follow up. Every time.

After every provider session, we check in. Not with a survey. With a message from someone who genuinely wants to know whether the support is helping.

"The families I see through Your Village are better supported than almost any I've worked with in fifteen years of practice. Someone has already listened to them, worked out the priorities, and given them permission to accept help. By the time I walk through the door, half the work is done."

— Sarah M., Postpartum Doula, Inner Melbourne

You tell us what's hard. We sort the rest.

Your coordinator draws on a vetted network of providers across four areas of postpartum support. You don't need to research any of them. That's our job.

Recovery and feeding

Doulas, lactation support, and pelvic floor physiotherapy — the professionals who help your body heal and help feeding work.

Home and daily life

Meals, household help, and the practical support that keeps everything from falling apart while you focus on your baby.

Sleep

Gentle, experienced sleep consultants and night nannies who work with your parenting approach, not against it.

Emotional wellbeing

Perinatal psychologists and counsellors who understand the postpartum period. Because how you're feeling matters as much as how your baby is doing.

Your coordinator may also help with life admin — birth registration, childcare waitlists, navigating Medicare. Every family is different.

A few things we want you to know.

Needing help doesn't mean you're failing.

It means you're going through one of the most physically and emotionally demanding transitions a person can experience. The mothers who reach out aren't the ones who can't cope. They're the ones who understand that doing it alone was never the plan.

There is no wrong time to ask.

Whether you're still pregnant and planning ahead, or weeks in and barely holding it together — this is the right time. We meet you wherever you are.

You don't need to know what you need.

That's literally what we're here for. The most common thing we hear is some version of "I don't even know where to start." We'll figure it out together.

All families are welcome here.

All birth experiences. All feeding choices. All family structures. No judgement. Only support.
